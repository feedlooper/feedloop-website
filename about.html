ABOUT

We’re building the infrastructure layer for a circular food system.

FeedLoop partners with organic waste generators to divert pre-consumer organics and convert them into valuable products—protein, oils, and fertilizer—reducing disposal burden while strengthening local supply chains.

What problem are we solving?

Waste is a growing cost center

Organics disposal is expensive and operationally painful. Regulations and climate policy are making “landfill as default” less viable each year.

Protein inputs are volatile

Fishmeal supply is capped and expensive, while soy supply chains face deforestation and import risk. Domestic alternatives are needed.

Our model

1) Secure waste partners

We accept consistent pre-consumer organics (food processing, brewery, produce, bakery). Gate fees turn waste into a paid input.

2) Convert with BSF

Black Soldier Fly larvae rapidly convert organics into protein and oils. Residual frass is processed into fertilizer.

3) Sell to high-value markets

We prioritize early markets where performance and sustainability command premium pricing (aquaculture, pet nutrition, specialty feed).

Why now

Policy + economics are aligning

Landfill constraints, carbon pricing, and corporate sustainability targets are creating a real economic incentive to divert organics.

Modular scaling is possible

Standardized reactor units allow repeatable deployment—so growth is driven by site replication, not bespoke engineering every time.
